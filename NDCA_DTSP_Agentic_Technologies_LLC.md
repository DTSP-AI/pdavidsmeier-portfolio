## 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore and/or engage in a business relationship involving the evaluation, development, licensing, deployment, integration, testing, support, investment in, or commercialization of Company's proprietary artificial intelligence systems, multi-agent architectures, software platforms, data assets, and related products and services (the "**Purpose**"). In connection with the Purpose, Company will disclose certain Confidential Information (defined below) to Recipient, and Recipient agrees to protect such information under the terms of this Agreement.

---

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"**Confidential Information**" means any and all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information — in any form, whether written, oral, visual, electronic, or machine-readable, and whether or not marked as "confidential" — disclosed or made available by Company (or observed, accessed, or derived by Recipient) before, on, or after the Effective Date, including without limitation:

**(a) Technology and Code.** Source code, object code, binaries, build artifacts, repositories, commit history, branches, pull requests, configuration files, infrastructure-as-code, deployment scripts, CI/CD pipelines, and development environments.

**(b) AI/ML Assets.** Multi-agent architectures, LangGraph/LangChain graph definitions, agent contracts, system prompts, prompt templates, prompt engineering methodologies, fine-tuning datasets, embeddings, vector stores, retrieval strategies, memory architectures (including layered cache/vector/database designs), tool definitions, tool orchestration logic, routing logic, model selection strategies, and evaluation harnesses.

**(c) Data Assets.** Training data, synthetic data, labeled datasets, knowledge bases, ontologies, knowledge graphs, embeddings, customer conversations, call transcripts, meeting recordings, telemetry, logs, and derivative datasets.

**(d) Trade Secrets.** Any information that derives independent economic value, actual or potential, from not being generally known and is the subject of reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy, as defined under the **Florida Uniform Trade Secrets Act, Fla. Stat. §§ 688.001–688.009**, and the federal **Defend Trade Secrets Act, 18 U.S.C. § 1836 et seq.**

**(e) Business Information.** Business plans, strategies, roadmaps, pricing, margins, financial statements, forecasts, pipelines, customer and prospect lists, vendor relationships, partnership terms, investor materials, cap tables, and marketing plans.

**(f) Product Information.** Product designs, specifications, prototypes, demos, feature sets, user experience flows, wireframes, architecture diagrams, API contracts, schemas, and performance benchmarks.

**(g) Intellectual Property.** Inventions, discoveries, improvements, know-how, methods, processes, techniques, algorithms, formulas, trade dress, unregistered and pending patents, provisional applications, and any IP filings or disclosures shared in connection with the Purpose.

**(h) Third-Party Information.** Any information Company has received from customers, clients, partners, or vendors under an obligation of confidentiality.

**(i) Derivatives.** All notes, analyses, summaries, compilations, studies, reports, and other materials prepared by Recipient that contain, reflect, or are derived from any of the foregoing.

### 2.1 Exclusions
Confidential Information does **not** include information that Recipient can demonstrate by contemporaneous written records:
(i) was lawfully in Recipient's possession, free of any obligation of confidence, prior to disclosure by Company;
(ii) is or becomes publicly known through no act or omission of Recipient or its Representatives;
(iii) is lawfully received from a third party not under a confidentiality obligation to Company; or
(iv) is independently developed by Recipient without use of or reference to any Confidential Information.

The burden of proving an exclusion rests on Recipient.

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECIPIENT

Recipient shall:

**(a)** hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and protect it using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own most sensitive information, and in no event less than a **reasonable standard of care**;

**(b)** use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever;

**(c)** not disclose, publish, distribute, disseminate, reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, copy, reproduce, or make derivative works of the Confidential Information, in whole or in part, except as expressly authorized in writing by Company;

**(d)** not use the Confidential Information to compete with Company, to solicit Company's customers, employees, contractors, or partners, or to develop any product, service, model, agent, prompt, or system that is similar to or competitive with Company's offerings;

**(e)** restrict access to Confidential Information to Recipient's employees, officers, directors, attorneys, accountants, and contractors (collectively, "**Representatives**") who (i) have a strict need to know for the Purpose, (ii) have been informed of the confidential nature of the information, and (iii) are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement. Recipient is fully responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives;

**(f)** not remove, alter, or obscure any proprietary notices, labels, or markings on any Confidential Information;

**(g)** not input, upload, submit, or otherwise expose any Confidential Information to any third-party artificial intelligence, machine learning, large language model, or training system (including but not limited to public AI chatbots, code assistants, or model providers) without Company's prior written consent; and

**(h)** promptly notify Company in writing of any actual or suspected unauthorized use, disclosure, loss, or compromise of Confidential Information, and cooperate fully with Company to regain possession and prevent further unauthorized use.

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND OWNERSHIP

**4.1 No License.** All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of Company. Nothing in this Agreement grants Recipient any right, title, interest, license, or ownership — express or implied, by estoppel or otherwise — in or to any Confidential Information, patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, or other intellectual property of Company.

**4.2 Feedback and Derivative Work.** Any feedback, suggestions, ideas, improvements, modifications, enhancements, or derivative works that Recipient provides or develops relating to Company's Confidential Information, products, or services ("**Feedback**") shall be the sole and exclusive property of Company. Recipient hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Feedback, including all intellectual property rights therein, and agrees to execute any documents reasonably necessary to perfect such assignment.

**4.3 No Reverse Engineering.** Recipient shall not, and shall not permit any third party to, reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or attempt to derive the source code, architecture, algorithms, prompts, model weights, or training methodology of any Company system, whether accessed through API, binary, demo, or otherwise.

**4.4 Residuals Clause Disclaimed.** No "residuals" or "general knowledge retained in unaided memory" clause shall be implied into this Agreement. Recipient may not rely on memorization as a basis for use or disclosure.

## 5. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

If Recipient is required by law, subpoena, court order, or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information, Recipient shall (to the extent legally permitted) (a) provide Company with prompt written notice prior to disclosure so that Company may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, (b) cooperate with Company's efforts to obtain such protection, and (c) disclose only the minimum portion of Confidential Information strictly required, continuing to treat the balance as confidential.

---

## 6. TERM AND DURATION OF OBLIGATIONS

**6.1 Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice.

**6.2 Survival of Obligations.** Recipient's confidentiality and non-use obligations shall survive termination of this Agreement and shall remain in force:
(a) for **five (5) years** from the date of disclosure with respect to non–trade secret Confidential Information; and
(b) **in perpetuity** (for so long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law) with respect to any Confidential Information constituting a trade secret.

---

## 7. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

Upon Company's written request, or upon termination of this Agreement or conclusion of the Purpose, Recipient shall, at Company's option, promptly (and in no event later than ten (10) business days):
(a) return to Company all Confidential Information and all copies, notes, extracts, and derivatives thereof in its possession or control; or
(b) permanently destroy all such Confidential Information (including secure deletion from all electronic systems, backups, and cloud storage to the extent technically feasible) and certify such destruction in writing to Company, signed by an authorized officer.

Recipient may retain one archival copy solely for legal compliance purposes, which shall remain subject to this Agreement indefinitely.

---

## 8. NO SOLICITATION

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of **twenty-four (24) months** thereafter, Recipient shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit for employment or engagement, hire, or otherwise engage any employee, contractor, or consultant of Company with whom Recipient became acquainted in connection with the Purpose, without Company's prior written consent. General solicitations not specifically targeted at Company personnel are permitted.

---

## 9. REMEDIES AND ENFORCEMENT

**9.1 Irreparable Harm.** Recipient acknowledges that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement would cause Company irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, Company shall be entitled to seek **injunctive relief, specific performance, and other equitable remedies**, without the necessity of posting a bond or proving actual damages, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

**9.2 Damages.** Company shall be entitled to recover all damages arising from a breach, including without limitation lost profits, disgorgement of profits obtained by Recipient through breach, exemplary damages where permitted by law, and reasonable **attorneys' fees and costs**.

**9.3 Trade Secret Enforcement.** Company expressly reserves all rights and remedies available under the Florida Uniform Trade Secrets Act, the Defend Trade Secrets Act, and all other applicable federal and state laws, including the right to seek **ex parte seizure** where authorized under 18 U.S.C. § 1836(b)(2).

**9.4 Notice of Immunity (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)).** Recipient is notified that under the Defend Trade Secrets Act, an individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that: (i) is made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official or to an attorney solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

## 13. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**13.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding its subject matter and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous agreements, understandings, and communications, whether written or oral.

**13.2 Amendments.** No modification or amendment of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

**13.3 Waiver.** No failure or delay by Company in exercising any right under this Agreement shall constitute a waiver of that right. No waiver shall be effective unless in writing and signed by Company.

**13.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be reformed to the minimum extent necessary to render it enforceable while preserving its original intent.

**13.5 Assignment.** Recipient may not assign, transfer, or delegate this Agreement or any of its rights or obligations, in whole or in part, without Company's prior written consent. Any purported assignment in violation of this section is void. Company may assign this Agreement freely, including in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets.

**13.6 Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by (a) personal delivery, (b) nationally recognized overnight courier, (c) certified mail (return receipt requested), or (d) email with confirmed delivery, to the addresses set forth above or such other address as a Party may designate in writing.

**13.7 Independent Contractors.** The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates any agency, partnership, joint venture, or employment relationship.

**13.8 Counterparts and Electronic Signatures.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one instrument. Electronic and digital signatures (including DocuSign, Adobe Sign, and scanned PDFs) shall be deemed valid and binding.

**13.9 Headings.** Section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect interpretation.

**13.10 Construction.** The Parties acknowledge that each has had the opportunity to review this Agreement with counsel, and any rule of construction to the effect that ambiguities are resolved against the drafting Party shall not apply.
